About TJCN

The TJ Computational Neuroscience Club meets every Wednesday during B-block. We discuss traditional Computational Neuroscience theory and attempt to elucidate connections between it and current research in science and cognition. We will host competitions to provide an opportunity for application, and will use problem sets with solutions to provide practice. We hope that you not only get something out of the meetings but also gain an appreciation for a field which underlies much of modern CS advancement today.

Past Officers Cirriculum Overview

In the beginning we will explore foundational Computational Neuroscience topics, often using mathematics to explain why our brain does what it does. We do not expect you have an advanced mathematics background, so we will cover all the necessary prerequisite material as we proceed.

From there, we will explore psychology and cognition, and why what we learned actually matters.

Finally, the club will conclude by delving into the Machine Learning applications of the content, including but not limited to medical imaging, and neurodegenerative disease diagnosis.

Resources

MITOpenCourseWare — A brief synopsis of introductory CN topics. Prior math knowledge is helpful.

University of Texas Notes — A great synopsis for neural encoding and decoding. It's especially useful in the conceptual aspects, but can get mathematically dense at some points. 

Neuroscience Textbook — Good reference resource to understand the biology of the nervous system.

Google Crash Course Machine Learning — Useful course to follow if you are interested in the application aspects of what we will talk about towards the end of the year.
